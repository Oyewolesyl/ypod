research logic

the ear is not marketing. it is the constraint.

why current earbuds fail

daytime hardware in a sleeping body.

Normal bluetooth earbuds are made for movement, calls, touch control, and lifestyle use. Sleep creates different forces: pillow pressure, ear canal angle, rotation, long wear time, heat, and accidental touch.

what must be studied

anatomy before renders.

Concha dimensions, cymba concha, cavum concha, tragus, anti-tragus, ear canal angle, shell protrusion, nozzle angle, and pillow contact all affect whether the product can actually be slept in.

category map

competitors and signals.

Study Ozlo, Soundcore Sleep, QuietOn, Kokoon, Apple, Samsung, Sony, and JBL. The opportunity is not just audio quality. It is sleep comfort, identity, personalization, and bedroom ritual.

study 1

concha anatomy

Real ear, no branding. Highlight concha, cymba concha, cavum concha, tragus, anti-tragus, and ear canal to understand available space.

study 2

yema-1 fit

Place current YEMA-1 in the ear and ask: how much protrudes, what touches the pillow first, and where pressure concentrates.

study 3

pressure simulation

Show ear, pillow, and earbud in cross-section with pressure zones and compression zones. This turns design opinion into evidence.

study 4

shape exploration

Compare flat oval pebble, flattened circle, concha-shaped organic bean, and half-moon sleepbud in the same ear at the same scale.

product requirements

lock the family before scaling the visuals.

yema-1

the locked circular model.

A simple sleep earbud: circular face, flat front disc, uniform thickness, soft edge radius, matte black body, subtle deeper-black ypod logo, soft silicone tip, no stem, no visible earbud LEDs.

yema pro

the researched side-sleeper model.

A premium low-profile sleep earbud built from anatomy research: flatter pebble/oval form, wider pressure distribution, deeper concha seating, and angled nozzle following the ear canal.

logo system

quiet branding.

Logos are text marks: ypod and yema. Black product uses a deeper black logo. Grey version uses deeper grey. No loud print, no bright mark.

future render rules

same geometry every time.

Future images must preserve silhouette, body proportions, nozzle dimensions, edge radius, face shape, logo placement, and material language.

image set needed

from research to launch.

Front, side, rear, floating pair, in-ear shot, case shot, exploded view, website hero, pressure simulation, concha study, and in-ear comparison.

validation

what must be proven.

Comfort over 6-8 hours, heat, rotation during sleep, pillow pressure, tip sizing, hygiene, battery behavior, and phone-first control acceptance.

remote and 3d customizer

ypod remote

sleep control without touching your ears.

A small bedside controller for pause, play, next, previous, sleep sound control, and a dream button that starts a saved bedtime routine.

dream button

one press for the night preset.

It can trigger a saved sound, preferred volume, sleep timer, fade-out, or favorite night preset. This makes ypod feel like a system.

custom wraps

make the remote part of the room.

Matte black is the base. Wrapped editions and user-uploaded designs create collectible personalization without making the brand childish.

1. generate

image to GLB

Use Hunyuan3D or another image-to-3D tool to create remote.glb from the black remote reference. Pick geometry, not texture quality.

2. correct

code materials

Treat the GLB as geometry only. Override body material with matte black, high roughness, low metalness, subtle studio lighting, and shadows.

3. decal

clean icons and logo

Do not trust generated logo geometry. Apply play/pause, previous, left, right, LED dot, and subtle ypod logo as clean overlays.

4. wrap

texture swapping

Apply matte black, Kuromi, gothic, minimal, and user-uploaded textures to the front face or fallback overlay plane.

viewer

floating 3D remote, slow auto-rotate, drag rotation, dark studio scene, lazy loading.

wrap controls

thumbnail selector, selected wrap name, reset, upload JPG/PNG, preview only.

fallback

if UV mapping fails, keep GLB matte black and place a rounded overlay plane above the face.

cost, bom and pricing

the cheapest smart path is validation before full custom electronics.

recommended launch-cost strategy

start with ODM samples.

Buy and test existing sleepbud ODM/OEM platforms first. Validate comfort, battery, app behavior, and brand demand before paying for full custom electronics.

prototype range

lean vs serious build.

Lean path: sample orders, concept renders, basic CAD, appearance prototype, and Codex/app MVP. Serious path: engineering support, compliance pre-checks, industrial design, DFM, and production planning.

hidden costs

budget beyond the render.

Certification, battery shipping, warranty reserve, returns, replacement tips, support, app maintenance, Bluetooth bugs, firmware updates, and phone OS changes all affect the real business.

core earbuds $129-$179

launch MSRP target for YPOD Sleepbuds Core.

wraps, premium packaging, and personalization lift perceived value.

bom target

ex-factory can range from lean ODM to premium custom. Avoid a high-cost custom V1 until demand is proven.

remote cost

prototype remote cost may be high; production target drops when housing, PCB, battery, and assembly scale.

margin risk

premium bundle margin can work, but high custom costs can destroy the business if MSRP cannot rise.

investor use

all numbers are planning estimates until supplier quotes, compliance scope, and order quantity are confirmed.

manufacturing and rfq

ask factories the questions that protect the product.

phase 1

source existing sleepbud ODM samples and compare comfort, runtime, charging, and app behavior.

phase 2

request private-label changes: logo, color, packaging, sound library naming, and app skin if available.

phase 3

develop a custom industrial design shell and charging case while preserving proven electronics.

phase 4

build the YPOD Remote as a separate accessory after the sleepbud core is validated.

supplier proof

verify the factory.

Ask for factory name, business license, export markets, product references, factory video tour, ISO/QC systems, and whether the mold is existing, private, or shared.

comfort proof

do not accept vague claims.

Request earbud weight, max thickness, side-sleeper test method, tip/wing sizes, material hardness, and actual overnight comfort data.

battery proof

runtime must be real.

Ask for Bluetooth runtime at 50% volume, sleep/local mode runtime, case capacity, charge cycles, battery supplier, and UN38.3 summary.

feature proof

separate real from marketing.

Confirm streaming, local sounds, private alarm, timer, find-my-earbud, sleep tracking, OTA firmware, ANC, and whether snore masking is adaptive or just playback.

quality checklist

test like a product.

Pairing cycles, 8-10 hour playback burn-in, dock/undock charging tests, pillow pressure test, audio balance, drop test, moisture test, packaging check.

compliance checklist

avoid selling blind.

FCC, CE/RED, RoHS, REACH, Bluetooth SIG, UN38.3, SDS/MSDS, lithium shipping rules, WEEE/recycling labels, and no medical claims.
