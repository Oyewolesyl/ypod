# ypod / yema sleep-earbud research brief

## Brand idea

ypod is a sleep-first bluetooth earphone brand. The feeling is intimate, quiet, matte, and low-logo. The product should feel like something made for night routines, love, privacy, and people who fall asleep with sound.

Tagline: `in my dream`

## Core customer problem

People already sleep with music, rain sounds, brown noise, prayers, podcasts, meditations, and comfort audio. Normal earbuds are not designed for sleep. Common issues:

- Stems and bulky shells press into the pillow.
- Touch controls trigger accidentally when the user turns over.
- Bright LEDs and loud branding feel wrong in a bedroom.
- Circular shells can create pressure at one point during side sleeping.
- Fit instability gets worse when the head moves against fabric.

## Product answer

YEMA-1 is the locked iconic model: simple circular face, matte black, no stem, soft silicone tip, low-contrast ypod logo.

YEMA PRO is the sleep-optimized model: same family DNA, but flatter and more oval so it can sit deeper inside the concha and distribute pillow pressure across a wider contact area.

## Design principles

- Low outward profile matters more than visual thinness.
- Soft material contact matters more than hard shell minimalism.
- Concha stability matters more than tight ear-canal pressure.
- Phone-first control is part of the comfort promise.
- The logo should be nearly invisible: emboss/deboss in deeper black or deeper grey.

## Product system

### yema-1

- Perfect circular face
- Flat front disc
- Uniform thickness
- Same edge radius in every render
- Matte black finish
- Silicone tip
- No stem
- No visible earbud LEDs
- Centered lowercase `ypod` mark

### yema pro

- Low-profile oval face
- Slightly wider contact surface
- Deeper concha seating
- Angled nozzle
- Same matte black or grey finish family
- Same quiet lowercase logo behavior

## Business pitch

ypod should not be pitched as another earbud. It is a sleep comfort brand. The first campaign should show:

- The problem: people use sound to sleep, but their earbuds are made for daytime.
- The emotional insight: sleep audio is private and intimate.
- The hardware insight: side-sleeper comfort is a geometry problem.
- The brand edge: quiet object, no accidental controls, no loud logo.
- The rollout: lock YEMA-1 as the icon, prove YEMA PRO through fit studies.

## Claims to validate before manufacturing

- Actual YEMA PRO height from ear surface under pillow compression
- Ear fit across small, medium, and large concha sizes
- Silicone tip comfort over 6 to 8 hours
- Rotation during sleep movement
- Battery heat during all-night use
- Buttonless/phone-first workflow acceptability
- Cleaning, wax, and hygiene behavior

## Source direction

- Soundcore Sleep A20: category reference for side-sleeper positioning, sleep mode, bluetooth mode, and longer battery claims.
- Ozlo Sleepbuds: category reference for all-night buds, silicone fit, low profile, and sleep-first streaming.
- WHO environmental noise guidelines: context for why night noise and sleep disturbance are a real health and comfort problem.
